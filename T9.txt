1. Appeal System Against Wrong Negative Fame

Idea:
Users should be able to appeal negative fame entries. If a user loses fame because a post was incorrectly rated as false or 
misleading, they should be able to request a review.

Why it is useful:
This would make the system fairer, because automatic ratings or the “Magic AI” can make mistakes.

Critical impact:
Without an appeal system, the network could become unfair and dangerous, because one wrong classification could have long-term 
consequences for a user’s reputation. However, an appeal system could also be abused if users constantly challenge every 
negative rating.

2. Community Moderation by High-Fame Users

Idea:
Users with very high fame levels, such as Super Pro or higher, could help moderate posts within their expertise area.

Why it is useful:
This means that questionable content would not only be judged by algorithms, but also by users who have proven expertise in 
the relevant topic.

Critical impact:
This could improve the quality of discussions, but it could also create a concentration of power. High-fame users might abuse 
their position or suppress unpopular opinions.

3. Fame Decay Over Time

Idea:
Fame should slowly decay over time if a user does not actively contribute to an expertise area anymore.

Why it is useful:
Knowledge can become outdated. For example, someone who was highly skilled in databases 15 years ago is not automatically 
still an expert in modern database systems today.

Critical impact:
This would make fame profiles more realistic, but it could also be unfair to users who are still knowledgeable but do not 
post frequently.

4. Topic Confidence Instead of Hard Decisions

Idea:
The Magic AI should not only return expertise areas and truth ratings, but also a confidence score.

Example:

Databases: Truthful, confidence 0.92
Medicine: Negative, confidence 0.41

Why it is useful:
If the confidence score is low, the system should react more carefully and not immediately punish the user by lowering their fame.

Critical impact:
This could reduce unfair punishments caused by uncertain AI decisions. However, it would also make the system more complex, and 
users would need to understand what confidence scores mean.

5. Positive Fame for High-Quality Contributions

Idea:
The system should not only punish users for spreading false information, but also reward users for correct and useful posts.

Why it is useful:
This would motivate users to contribute high-quality content instead of only avoiding negative fame.

Critical impact:
This could make the network more constructive and less punishment-focused. However, it could also lead to “fame farming”, 
where users post many simple but correct statements only to increase their fame.
